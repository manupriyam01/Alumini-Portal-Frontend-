NSS

National Service Scheme

The National Service Scheme (NSS) is an Indian government initiative aimed at encouraging students to take part in community service and contribute to societal development. Established in 1969 by the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, NSS provides a platform for students to engage in social welfare activities while fostering a sense of responsibility, leadership, and empathy. The scheme promotes volunteerism by encouraging students to work on projects like health camps, sanitation drives, and literacy programs, particularly in rural areas. By involving students in social service, NSS helps them understand the challenges faced by underprivileged communities and develop a deeper sense of social responsibility. Through participation, volunteers not only contribute to the betterment of society but also gain valuable life skills such as teamwork, communication, and problem-solving. The NSS also conducts special camps in rural areas, where students actively contribute to community development through various projects. The program’s focus on personal growth and nation-building empowers students to become responsible citizens. Volunteers are awarded certificates upon completing the required hours, which add value to their academic and professional portfolios. Ultimately, NSS plays a crucial role in shaping the character of students, preparing them for life beyond academics, and creating an informed, responsible youth ready to serve the nation.The NSS motto is “Not me, not you, but we”The NSS motto expresses the essence of democratic living and upholds the need for service, an appreciation of another person’s point of view and consideration for fellow human beings. It illustrates that the welfare of an individual is ultimately dependent on the welfare of society as a whole; therefore, it is the aim of the NSS to demonstrate its motto in day-to-day activities The SJM Polytechnic also has NSS Activity and functioning as well.
